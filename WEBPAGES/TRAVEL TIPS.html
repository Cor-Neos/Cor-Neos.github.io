Best Times to Travel to Cebu

Every country has its best times to visit for tourists, which depends on geographical conditions and the unique cultural customs of each. For example, the land of the four seasons is most interesting to visit in spring. The Philippines, an island country in Southeast Asia, has two seasons. The best time to visit for activities like swimming or going to the beach is from January to April. During these months, there are more sunny days. However, if you are the type who prefers to travel during the low season, then come from July to October. The atmosphere in Cebu will be more relaxed than usual during this period. Not only that, but the prices of goods there were lower.

Use ‘GRAB’ and White Metered Taxis

Just like anywhere in the Philippines, transportation in Cebu is a chaotic mess. If you stay in the city, the most convenient way to get around is booking a ride on the GRAB app. GRAB is just like Uber, but the South East Asian version of it. You can key in your credit card if you want to go cashless, or pay the driver in cash. Booking a car is almost immediate, unless it’s rush hour (7AM to 9AM and 5PM to 8PM), which might cause some delays. If for whatever reason you cannot book a Grab, hail a white metered taxi. There are different taxi operators in the city, but the white ones tend to be more honest than the others.

Prepare to Eat Lots of Meat

Filipinos are fond of meat, especially pork and chicken, more so in Cebu. The province’s most popular dish is lechon, which is a full roast pig. Next to it is chicharron, which is deep fried pork rind. Meat dishes come in all forms and sizes – stewed, fried, barbecued, roasted. Of course, everything comes with a serving of rice. If you really want to go local, then order the “puso”, which is rice cooked in banana leaves. Needless to say, vegans, vegetarians, or anyone on any special diet might have a difficult time sitting down for a meal in Cebu. There are special vegan and vege-friendly restaurants popping up, but you’ll have to go out of your way to find them.

Bring Cash

Bringing cash when traveling to the Philippines is advisable due to the widespread use of cash in various regions, especially in smaller towns and rural areas where card acceptance may be limited. Local transportation, such as tricycles and jeepneys, often prefers cash payments, and having physical currency proves beneficial in emergency situations or areas with limited access to ATMs. Additionally, using cash helps avoid transaction fees associated with international card usage, and it aligns with the cultural practices of some smaller businesses and markets that rely on cash transactions for simplicity.

Where To Stay In Cebu

Cebu's diverse hotel options cater to various preferences and budgets. Budget-conscious travelers can find comfortable yet affordable options. The town center hosts luxurious hotels with top-notch services, dining, and spas, while bustling neighborhoods offer mid-range accommodations showcasing Cebuano hospitality. Conveniently located, these hotels provide easy access to attractions and historical sites.
Luxury Hotels = Average price: $200 – $500 per night. Medium Range Hotels = Average price: $100 – $200 per night.
Affordable Hotels = Average price: $40 – $80 per night.

Consider The Weather

Cebu's tropical climate, characterized by warm temperatures year-round, offers a delightful environment for travelers. The dry season, extending from January to April, ensures sun-soaked days perfect for beach outings and outdoor exploration, while the wet season, spanning from June to November, brings occasional rains, providing a unique charm to the landscape and an opportunity to witness the lush greenery that flourishes during this period.

Get Prepared for Festivals and Events

Check the local calendar for festivals and events happening during your visit. Cebu is known for vibrant festivals such as Sinulog, celebrated with lively street dances and cultural performances. Explore the enchanting cultural tapestry of Cebu by joining the Sinulog festival, a celebration that blends traditional dances and lively performances, offering a captivating glimpse into the rich heritage of the region.
